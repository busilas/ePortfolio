04 MODULE

Network Security.

As a student in the MSc Computer Science program, I have undertaken a comprehensive module of Network Security aimed at mastering the intricacies of network security. This e-portfolio documents my journey through the module, capturing my learnings and experiences across various units. Using the analogy of a "soundboard"—a tool used by sound engineers to control and balance audio signals—I reflect on how each unit equipped me to fine-tune my knowledge and skills in network security.

Throughout the Network Security module, I navigated various aspects of securing networks, akin to mastering the controls of a complex soundboard. Each unit provided valuable insights and hands-on experience, equipping me with the knowledge and skills to address contemporary and future network security challenges.

Main gols for the module:

1. Develop the ability to pinpoint security threats and vulnerabilities within network systems.

2. Learn to determine appropriate methodologies, tools, and techniques to manage or resolve these issues.

3. Design and evaluate computer programs and systems aimed at managing and auditing risk and security issues.

4. Collect and integrate information from various sources, including internet security alerts and warning sites, to systematically analyze security breaches and issues.

5. Understand and articulate the legal, social, ethical, and professional challenges faced by information security and risk professionals.

  01 UNIT

History of Network Security, Vulnerabilities, and Approaches

Required Reading

In the first unit, I delved into the history of network security, tracing the origins and evolution of vulnerabilities and attacks. The Cyber Kill Chain model proposed by Lockheed Martin was a focal point, providing a framework to understand and mitigate contemporary breach approaches.

Just like adjusting the various knobs and sliders on a soundboard to get the perfect sound mix, understanding the history and evolution of network security allowed me to fine-tune my knowledge and approach towards securing networks.
